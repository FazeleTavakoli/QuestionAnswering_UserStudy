This survey is designed to study if the question-answering system interprets the user's question (expressed in a natural language) in terms of SPARQL queries for the DBpedia knowledge graph, in a correct way or not.

Below you can see a general schema of this survey.

Step 1

To start the survey, first you should look at the part 1. It shows the question in natural language.

Step 2

Then you should look at the part 2, which shows the corresponding answer.

Step 3

On the part 3, you will see one of the four different forms of question interpretation, including sparql query, knowledge graph, verbalized answer, or controlled language, on the part 3.

Step 4

On the part 4, the system asks you if the question makes sense to you or not. You should consider question, answer, and interpretation, and then complete this step. You can also skip the question. From a drop down list you can choose the skipping reason. In case you choose "other" as the reason, you should also provide your own reason.
